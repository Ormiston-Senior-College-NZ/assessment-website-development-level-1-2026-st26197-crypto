Life's Purpose II

We do not make decisions randomly; we make them based on motivations.

Our motivations always support decisions that either push us towards something desirable or pulls us away from something undesirable.

Therefore, our decisions are made with the intention to maximise pleasure and minimise suffering.

Our goals are achieved to achieve other goals. However, there must be one that all others aim to achieve. This goal is to maximise pleasure and minimise suffering.

For example, you go to school not for the sake of it; you go to school to learn. You learn not for the sake of it; you learn to apply your knowledge. You apply knowledge not for the sake of it; you apply knowledge to survive. And you survive not for the sake of it; you survive to be happy or experience happiness.

Some may argue that survival is more important than happiness, but I believe that even a few minutes of happiness are worth more than an eternity of suffering. This is not to encourage suicide; people in those places must try to find the things that make them happier than they are depressed or suicidal, even if it may not be easy.
